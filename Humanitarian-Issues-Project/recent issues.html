Syrian Crisis

As one of the most severe humanitarian crises, the Syrian Crisis has displaced millions of Syrian refugees over the world. After the Syrian Civil War began, hundreds of thousands of Syrians have been killed through brutal conflict. It has been said that almost 11.1 million people need humanitarian assistance due to the destruction of facilities for schools, hospitals, utilities, and water and sanitation systems. Read more

Yemen Crisis

Considered the largest humanitarian crisis, the Yemen Crisis has led to 24 million people in need of humanitarian assistance. As a result of the Yemen Civil War, the crisis has forced millions of people to leave their homes. The conflict has destroyed public infrastructure and homes. Malnutrition rates and water shortages have increased dramatically with children suffering the most in this crisis. Read more

Democratic Republic of Congo Crisis

The Republic of Congo Crisis is one of the largest in the world with over five million people displaced within its borders. As a result of the long-running Civil War which ended in 2003, conflict all over the country has led to political instability, damage to infrastructure, as well as human rights violations. Thirteen million people are in need of humanitarian assistance, lacking food, water, or shelter. Read more

Afghanistan Crisis

The result of long-lasting conflict, the Afghanistan Crisis has affected millions of people in Afghanistan. The Taliban-ruled country is suffering economically due to conflict over the past few years. Closed borders are preventing aid from organizations like the UN and over five million people are in need of humanitarian assistance due to famine. Violence has caused the destruction of infrastructure and the deaths of thousands of citizens. Read more

South Sudan Crisis

The South Sudan Crisis, resulting from the Civil War, has lead to the deaths of almost four hundred thousand people. Over four million people have fled the country due to raging conflict and crimes against humanity in this political struggle. Over seven million people are in a dire need of humanitarian assistance while living in desperate situations. The main causes being food shortages and lack of shelter. Read more

Somalian Crisis

The Somalian Crisis is a result of not only violent conflict but also extreme weather conditions and disease. Government violence and abuses have sparked violence and protests over the country. Severe drought and floods have damaged infrastructure causing widespread famine and disease to spread. Over two million people are displaced within its borders and are in need of humanitarian assistance. Read more
